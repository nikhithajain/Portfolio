Description

Dementia is a syndrome in which there is deterioration in memory, thinking, behavior and the ability to perform everyday activities. Alzheimer is a type of Dementia that affects memory, thinking and behavior. Since the symptoms of Alzheimer and Dementia are almost similar, it is difficult to differentiate between the two. The analysis of structural and functional multi-modal images such as Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI), Computed Tomography (CT), Positron Emission Tomography (PET), Single-Photon Emission Computed Tomography (SPECT) of the brain may provide quantitative evidence of different neuro-degenrative diseases. The aim is to fuse the multi-modal images pertaining to Alzheimer and Dementia and to classify them with the help of Machine Learning and Deep Learning algorithms, compare the performace metrices and choose the best method. Two fusion methods used are: Discrete Wavelet Transformation (DWT) and Multi-resolution Singular Value Decomposition (MSVD). The Machine Learning algorithms used are: K-Nearest Neighbour (k-NN) and Support Vector Machine (SVM). The Deep Learning algorithm used for image classification is: Convolution Neural Network (CNN).
